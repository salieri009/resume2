# Concept Sheet · L3 / A-104 — The House

> SITE 009 · Stage 2 concept art document · derived from
> [`docs/bible/04-ROOMS/L3-LAB-GUNDAM.md`](../bible/04-ROOMS/L3-LAB-GUNDAM.md) — no redesign, only fixation.
> Station: LAB GUNDAM (raised mirror diagonal from the south-west · gaze at the door line). Tone scale 0–10.

## Composition

Mirror-raised diagonal, elevation ≈ 33 degrees. The smallest plinth in
the laboratories, 1.6 × 1.6 m, mostly empty: the tower stands alone at
its center with generous bare resin around it — the air is the
composition. The three stories stack in clear silhouette; the spine
conduit runs the tower's height at its front face; the lockset block
sits astride the spine at the middle story, small but centered in the
frame's focus. The eye enters at the resin top story (brightest mass),
drops down the spine, pauses involuntarily at the lockset (the only
interruption on the line), and lands on the half-sunk foundation.

## Architecture

**Foundation** — PostgreSQL: concrete slab 0.7 × 0.25 × 0.55 m, sunk
half its height into the plinth (0.125 m proud). **Middle story** —
Chalice: aluminum floor 0.55 × 0.35 × 0.45 m, floating: its underside
held 60 mm above the foundation's top with *nothing* beneath — a
continuous dark shadow-gap line, no posts. **Top story** — Next.js:
resin volume 0.5 × 0.45 × 0.4 m with one wide opening scored on its
south face. **Spine**: a single hairline riser running top-center to
foundation down the tower's front. **Lockset** — JWT: aluminum block
90 × 60 × 40 mm mounted ON the spine at the Chalice story, the conduit
entering its top face and leaving its bottom face. Caption beneath the
door line: `AUTH · GOOGLE OAUTH → SERVER VERIFY → SHORT-LIVED JWT →
REQUEST CHECK`.

## Light

Three stories, three values: resin top tone 9, aluminum middle tone 7
with lengthwise sheen, concrete foundation tone 6.5 — the stack legible
in silhouette alone. The floating gap is the light's signature: an
unbroken tone-4.5 line across the tower's base, the darkest mark in the
room, interrupted by no post. Plinth top tone 9; the tower's single
shadow falls short and doubled to the north-west.

## Mood

Modest and rigorous — a summer house built by one person who refused
shortcuts on the hard parts. Earned by the emptiness around the tower
(no wings, no outbuildings), by the shortest tool rack in the building
presented without padding, and by the held pause at the lock.

## Interaction States — Three Plates

**Plate 1 · Resting.** Tower and spine in graphite and material tones;
gap line dark; no blue.

**Plate 2 · Hover — the checked descent.** Boundary heavy signal; the
trace runs the spine top → down, **holds one readable beat at the
lockset** (the blue stopped inside the block), then continues to the
foundation. Leader note `ROOM · L2 · A-104 · GUNDAM`.

**Plate 3 · Detail — the gap.** Tight crop of the tower's base at the
same angle: foundation top, the unbroken shadow-gap line, the floating
floor's underside — no caption. The plate exists to fix the fact that
nothing holds the middle story up, and that the drawing says so
without a word.

## References

Case-study-house axonometrics (one small volume, honest structure);
Rams' 606 shelving for the floating-off-the-wall reading; a mortise
lock's exploded diagram for the lockset-on-spine; Japanese storehouse
(kura) proportions for the tower. The pause: a metronome's caught
beat.
