# Concept Sheet · L2 / A-102 — The Order Warehouse

> Station: LAB IOTBAY (mirror family of the crowd station, approached from the south-west ·
> gaze at the racking midline · plinth fills the frame's lower two-thirds). Tone scale 0–10.

## Composition

The mirror of A-101's shot: plan angle ≈ 35 degrees off the south axis
from the *west* side, elevation ≈ 30 degrees — the frame reads
left-to-right down the aisles. The plinth is the longest in the building,
3.6 × 1.9 m, long axis left-to-right. Three parallel racks run the long
axis, front to back: interface nearest the viewer, logic behind it and
tallest, data deepest. The CI gantry stands at the frame's right edge,
perpendicular to the racks, with the shipped container block just past
it at the plinth's very edge. The Tailwind plate sits alone at the lower
left, visibly off the route. The eye enters at the tall logic rack,
runs right along the aisle rhythm, crosses the gantry's threshold
shadow, and lands on the lone container — reading the room as a flow
toward dispatch, which is the exhibit's whole argument.

## Architecture

Racks as aluminum shelving at model scale: interface rack 2.6 × 0.12 ×
0.35 m (a long low shelf face); logic rack 2.6 m long, two shoulder
masses (Servlets 0.5 × 0.6 × 0.4, Services 0.4 × 0.5 × 0.4) on a shared
rail; data rack: eight identical DAO bays, each 0.28 × 0.4 × 0.3 m on
strict 0.32 m centers — hairline dividers, no variation — with the
SQLite slab behind them, 2.4 × 0.3 × 0.5 m, the widest single mass.
Picking route: hairline conduit with drafting elbows entering at the
interface face, rising through logic, crossing to a DAO bay, landing on
the store. CI gantry: two uprights 0.15 × 1.1 m and a crossbeam
spanning 1.4 m, at the right end; under it the conveyor caption; past
it one container-proportioned block 0.5 × 0.25 × 0.25 m. Tailwind
plate: 0.35 × 0.06 × 0.35 m, flat, no connector.

## Light

Key from upper left rakes down the aisles: each rack drops a soft band
of tone-8 shadow onto the next lane — three parallel pale stripes, the
floor's logistical texture. The gantry, perpendicular, takes the key
across its beam and lays the longest, darkest line in the room (tone
7) — the threshold. The DAO bays each throw the same minute shadow,
eight identical marks. Plinth top tone 9; racks tone 6.5–7 with
lengthwise sheen; SQLite slab flattest, tone 7; container block catches
the key on its top edge, the frame's brightest small face at the right.

## Mood

Bureaucratic beauty — a floor that is proud of its paperwork. Earned by
repetition under raking light (eight identical bays, three identical
shadow bands), and by the single container at the dispatch edge: one
artifact that cleared inspection, resting where the room's whole flow
terminates.

## Interaction States — Three Plates

**Plate 1 · Resting.** All graphite; route and conveyor as hairlines;
boundary medium; no blue.

**Plate 2 · Hover — the picking route.** Boundary heavy signal; the
route traces interface → logic → DAO → SQLite in `diagram[]` order; the
gantry and its conveyor stay graphite. Leader note `ROOM · L2 · A-102 ·
IOTBAY`.

**Plate 3 · Hover — the gantry.** The room's second flow: the gantry's
own hover takes its uprights' outline to signal and runs the blue along
the conveyor caption line — push → GH Actions → 118 E2E → Docker image
→ GHCR — while the racks rest in graphite. Two flows, never both at
once.

## References

Warehouse racking elevations from logistics engineering drawings;
container-port gantry silhouettes (drawn, not photoreal); Bernd & Hilla
Becher's typological repetition for the DAO bays; the conveyor line as
a railway block diagram. Museum mount: a process vitrine — the room as
a working diagram of throughput.
