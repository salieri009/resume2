# Concept Sheet · R — The Roof

> SITE 009 · Stage 2 concept art document · derived from
> [`docs/bible/04-ROOMS/R-ROOF.md`](../bible/04-ROOMS/R-ROOF.md) — no redesign, only fixation.
> Station: ROOF (high frontal, the building below the frame's waist). Tone scale 0–10.

## Composition

The building's massing holds the frame's lower third; above and around
it, unbroken white — no grid visible at this height, no edge in frame:
the void becomes fog by running out of references. Near the roof's
center, plumb and facing the station, the identity plate on two short
posts. Nothing else on the lid. The eye falls to the plate because it
is the only thing left to read.

## Architecture

Plate: resin, door proportions 1.4 × 2.0 m on 40 mm posts, engraved in
graphite: call sign small (`SALIERI · SITE 009`), the name full-width
(JUNGWOOK VAN), the invitation and the one forward-looking line, then
four addresses in full text (a plaque is read, not scanned), and at the
foot the closing stamp `REVISION A · END OF SET`.

## Light / Mood

Key and fill merge into sourceless brightness (ground tone 9.5, sky
tone 9.5, indistinguishable); the plate's face flat tone 9.3; its two
post shadows short, doubled, almost apologetic. After nine rooms of
evidence: quiet.

## Interaction States — Three Plates

**Plate 1 · Arrival.** As above; no blue.

**Plate 2 · A door underlined.** One address carries the signal
underline; leader note `ROOM · R · OBS`. Doors leave the building
standing.

**Plate 3 · The farewell begun.** The teardown's first register:
materials surrendering to white massing below the plate, stamps still
present — they go last. Fixes the exit's order: solid → line → void,
reversed construction, ~4 s, the single ease.

## References

Dedication plaques (bronze, transcribed to resin); Hiroshi Sugimoto's
sea horizons for fog-by-absence; the last page of a drawing set.
