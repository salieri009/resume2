# Concept Sheet · L3 / A-105 — The Pavilion

> SITE 009 · Stage 2 concept art document · derived from
> [`docs/bible/04-ROOMS/L3-LAB-EPHEMERAL.md`](../bible/04-ROOMS/L3-LAB-EPHEMERAL.md) — no redesign, only fixation.
> Station: LAB EPHEMERAL (the one frontal lab — gallery presentation · gaze at the roof line). Tone scale 0–10.

## Composition

Frontal and centered — the only lab met head-on, elevation ≈ 28
degrees. The pavilion presents like a piece on a gallery floor:
near-square plinth 2.4 × 2.2 m, the structure centered, air on all
sides counted as part of the piece. The three roof planes stack in the
frame's upper half, their edges reading as three parallel lines with
visible air between; the undercroft's two courts sit in the lower half,
symmetric about the center line. The eye enters at the roof lamination
(the piece's signature), passes down the thinnest columns in the
building, and divides between the two courts — fluid left, clock right.

## Architecture

Structure: four aluminum posts 40 × 40 mm at 1.6 × 1.4 m centers,
1.1 m tall. **Roof**: three glass planes 1.8 × 1.6 m stacked with
80 mm air gaps — lowest at opacity ≈ 0.5 (bgLayer, frosted), middle ≈
0.35 with faint etched mottling (historyLayer), top ≈ 0.2, clearest
(activeLayer); eave caption `LAYERS · BGLAYER STATIC · HISTORYLAYER ·
ACTIVELAYER`. **Fluid court** (left): p5.js plate 0.4 × 0.08 × 0.25;
Perlin field mass 0.6 × 0.18 × 0.45, top face etched with combed
hairline strokes; object pool — 18 identical 50 mm blanks in a 6 × 3
rack. **Clock court** (right): audio block 0.25 × 0.2 × 0.2 and clock
block 0.25 × 0.25 × 0.2 side by side; IoC base slab 0.6 × 0.12 × 0.4
beneath them. Cross-conduit between courts at 0.35 m height; caption at
the clock: `HOURS · SUN DROP · QUARTERS · CYMATIC RINGS · 0 ASSETS`.

## Light

Triple-filtered: the softest light in the building, near-shadowless.
Each roof plane lays a faint edge-shadow on the one below — three
parallel hairlines of shade reading as the lamination's section, tones
8.5/8.7/8.9. The undercroft holds a pale pooled brightness, tone 8.8;
the pool's blanks catch it evenly, no glints. Masses tone 7–7.5. The
plinth beyond the roof's shadow returns to tone 9.

## Mood

A folly, engineered — the most delicate object in the building, framed
by the most rigorous architecture in the set. Earned by the lamination
(engineering as beauty), the rack of waiting blanks (pre-allocation as
a monument), and the fastest stillness in the site: when attention
leaves, nothing lingers, because stillness is the exhibit's subject.

## Interaction States — Three Plates

**Plate 1 · Resting.** Glass, aluminum, graphite; the etched flow-field
a change of sheen only; no blue, no motion — the resting pavilion is
the artwork's calm state, depicted.

**Plate 2 · Hover — attention arrives.** Boundary heavy signal; both
courts trace at once (p5 → Perlin → pool; clock → IoC), the
cross-conduit carrying the beat between them. Leader note `ROOM · L2 ·
A-105 · EPHEMERAL`. Roof planes do not answer; their caption does.

**Plate 3 · The instant after.** The identical frame to Plate 1 —
recorded as its own plate to fix the rule: the cooldown is
instantaneous, and the painter must know the "after" looks exactly
like the "before".

## References

SANAA's Serpentine Pavilion (thin members, roof as the whole
architecture); laminated-glass section drawings; letterpress type
cases for the pool rack; cymatics plates (Jenny) for the clock
caption's imagery — referenced, never rendered. The stillness: Morandi.
