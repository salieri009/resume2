# Concept Sheet · L4 — The Archive & The Library

> SITE 009 · Stage 2 concept art document · derived from
> [`docs/bible/04-ROOMS/L4-ARCHIVE-LIBRARY.md`](../bible/04-ROOMS/L4-ARCHIVE-LIBRARY.md) — no redesign, only fixation.
> Stations: ARCHIVE (raised diagonal at the drawer line) · LIBRARY (one pan west). Tone scale 0–10.

## Composition — Archive

The drawer wall fills the frame's center: a plan chest of two rows ×
four fronts, resin faces with aluminum pulls, lit flat and even (tone
9 faces, no raking drama). Four fronts carry embossed seals; the rest
are blank. One drawer may stand open a hand's width, its document
readable inside. The eye reads the seal row left to right in service
order: EIGHTH ARMY · ROK ARMY · 1 / 620 · UTS BUILD.

## Composition — Library

One pan west: the smallest habitable room — a single resin desk near
the aperture, the domestic patch of key light across its top (tone
9.5), one aluminum plate reading `350+ ESSAYS · KO · EN · JA`. Nothing
else.

## Architecture

Chest: 2.4 × 1.2 × 0.5 m; fronts 0.52 × 0.24 m, 40 mm proud when
closed, sliding +0.25 m when open (0.4 s, the single ease, one at a
time). Seals: 90 mm circles, raised, English always. Desk: 0.9 × 0.72
× 0.5 m, plain.

## Mood

The hush of records; the warmth of one reading light. The `1 / 620`
seal second from left — ordered by service date, not prestige.

## Interaction States — Three Plates

**Plate 1 · Archive resting.** All fronts closed, seals reading as
relief; flat light; no blue.

**Plate 2 · A drawer answers.** One front's outline heavy signal, the
drawer open a hand's width, the document flat inside; the previously
open drawer already eased shut. Leader note `ROOM · L4 · ARC`.

**Plate 3 · The library.** The desk under its patch of light, plate
underlined as a door (`SERVES` from the intercom riser resolving here).
Leader note `ROOM · L4 · LIB`.

## References

Plan chests in drawing registries; notary embossers; Shaker writing
desks; the National Archives reading-room light.
