# Concept Sheet · L0 — The Lobby

> SITE 009 · Stage 2 concept art document · derived from
> [`docs/bible/04-ROOMS/L0-LOBBY.md`](../bible/04-ROOMS/L0-LOBBY.md) — no redesign, only fixation.
> Station: LOBBY (14, 16, 14 · gaze 0, 1.2, 0 · zoom 36). Values below use a 0–10 tone scale
> (0 = `--ink`, 10 = white); the sheet itself sits at 9.5.

## Composition

The frame is a corner-on orthographic view of the building, taken from the
south-east and above: plan angle 45 degrees (the camera's x and z are
equal), elevation angle ≈ 39 degrees. Two faces are visible — the south
face with its glass curtain, facing the viewer's left-forward, and the
east face, shorter, falling away right — meeting at the near vertical
corner, which is the composition's strongest standing line. The building
occupies the central band of the frame, its mass centered slightly below
the geometric center so the paper field reads generously above the roof
plate; roughly one building-height of clear paper surrounds the mass on
all sides. The survey grid rules the field to the frame's edges, fading
in confidence outward.

The eye's route is fixed: it enters at the near corner (the highest
contrast junction — graphite edges against paper), slides left along the
glass curtain's top rail, passes *through* the glass to the thesis wall —
the brightest broad plane in frame, set parallel to the south face and
therefore facing the viewer at the same 45 degrees — reads the wall's
text block top to bottom, and settles at its base line. The floor rail of
the HUD occupies the frame's left margin outside the drawing; the corner
stamps hold the four corners at stamp size. No vanishing point exists;
depth is carried by three overlaps (curtain over floor plate, thesis wall
over rear wall, columns over both) and by the shadow gap outlining the
thesis wall.

## Architecture

Footprint 8 × 6 m; walls 120 mm at 3.4 m; ground slab 100 mm expressed as
a plinth edge; resin mid-plate 80 mm held inside the wall line with a
12 mm shadow gap; roof plate 120 mm flush to the wall line. The four
columns, 220 mm square, stand 0.4 m inside the corners — in this view the
near column overlaps the glass curtain's right third, and its full
ground-to-roof run must read unbroken. The glass curtain: 55 % of the
south face's width (4.4 m), 70 % of its height (2.38 m), seated on the
slab, a 40 mm aluminum frame line at head and sill. The thesis wall: 70 %
of the north face's width (5.6 m), 55 % of the wall height (1.87 m),
standing 80 mm proud of the concrete behind it. The lobby floor is
otherwise empty; near the curtain, the light patch (below) is the floor's
only event.

## Light

One sky: key from high south-east — in this frame, from the upper left,
almost along the view axis, so the two visible faces are the lit faces —
and cool fill from the north-west, arriving as the tone that keeps the
interior's shadowed planes legible. Tonal plan: paper field 9.5; roof
plate top 8; south and east wall faces 7.5; interior floor plate 9 where
the curtain's admitted light lies (a soft-edged rhomb, its shape the
curtain's outline sheared by the 39-degree sun, edges feathering over
50–80 mm at model scale), 8.5 elsewhere; thesis wall face 9 — the
brightest broad plane, a half-tone above the floor; concrete behind it
7; shadow gap around the thesis wall 5.5, the darkest *area* in the
frame (all true darks are lines). Column shadows: two short diagonals on
the interior floor, tone 8, doubled at their edges where key and fill
disagree. Nothing in the frame is darker than tone 5 except graphite
linework (2) and the title text (1).

## Materials

Concrete walls: matte, tone-flat, no texture at this scale; their only
modulation is the half-tone difference between the key-lit outer faces
and the fill-lit inner faces. Resin (floor plate, thesis wall): a
half-step warmer and brighter than concrete; where the light patch lies
on it, the highlight rolls off soft — no sheen line, only a broad lift.
Aluminum columns: vertical brushing rendered as a single lengthwise
gradient, tone 6 shading to 7.5 at the lit edge; no specular dot. Glass:
one flat tint layer, tone 8.5 with a blue-gray cast; everything seen
through it drops one half-tone and loses edge sharpness by one line
weight; no reflections of any kind on its surface — the drawing behind
it is the picture.

## Mood

Calm authority — the room where the argument is made once, quietly, by a
building that does not raise its voice. The mood is earned by emptiness
(one wall of words, a floor with nothing on it), by the highest tonal
floor in the building (nothing here is dark), and by the stillness of a
fixed sun: the light patch on the floor will never move, and its
permanence reads, after a few seconds, as composure.

## Interaction States — Three Plates

**Plate 1 · Resting.** As composed above. All lines graphite: grid at
hairline, footprint and mass edges at medium. The HUD rail's current
floor (`L0`) at full graphite, others at half-tone. Cursor: system arrow
over the void, crosshair only when it crosses an answering surface. No
blue anywhere in the frame.

**Plate 2 · Hover (the lab, seen from the lobby).** Identical frame; the
crowd laboratory's mass on the plate above takes its boundary at heavy
weight in signal `#2F6BFF` — the only saturated element in the picture —
and the leader note `ROOM · L2 · PROJECT 01 · CROWD` extends from its
upper edge on a hairline leader with a short horizontal landing, set in
the drafting mono at label size. The crosshair sits at the cursor. The
rest of the frame is unchanged: one blue boundary, one note, nothing
else woken.

**Plate 3 · The wall read (arrival state).** Same station, the visitor
settled: the thesis wall's text block rendered legible — kicker at stamp
size, title in ink (tone 1, the darkest marks in the plate), doctrine at
half the title's weight, tagline, five-line story paragraph, signature
line, and the single hairline-ruled button below. The button in its
resting state: graphite rule, no fill. This plate is the typographic
plate; its success is measured by whether the wall reads as *engraved on
an object standing in light* — the text must sit on the resin's tone 9,
not float on white.

## References

Composition and model presence: SANAA / Kazuyo Sejima 1:100 white
maquettes photographed on seamless paper; OMA competition model
photography (frontal calm, no dramatization). Light: Tadao Ando, Koshino
House — the drawn patch of sun on a bare interior plane as the room's
only event. Glass restraint: Mies, Barcelona Pavilion photographs where
glass reads as tint, not reflection. Wall-as-inscription: Sir John
Soane's Museum drawing-room labels; Otl Aicher's ruled information
plates. The button: Braun control-panel engraving (Rams era) — a rule
and a word, no bevel. Grid and stamps: any Foster + Partners working
drawing title block, taken literally.
